## Geospatial Understanding and Representation Alignment Requirements

This section captures requirements derived from SIGACTs that reveal challenges in understanding and aligning spatial information across different representations, such as maps, planned flight paths, and real-world observations. These SIGACTs highlight situations where operators must mentally translate between representations or manually manipulate artifacts to relate planned trajectories to what they observe in the environment.

The requirements focus on supporting operators in reasoning about aircraft position, movement, and adherence to planned routes by ensuring that spatial information can be interpreted in a way that aligns with their viewpoint and supports accurate judgment. The goal is to reduce cognitive burden associated with mental rotation or frame translation and to enable operators to confidently determine whether sUAS are behaving as expected.

Each entry documents the observed issue, an interpretation of the underlying spatial reasoning challenge, a main requirement, a derived requirement that refines or supports the capability, and an example non-functional requirement with a measurable outcome.
