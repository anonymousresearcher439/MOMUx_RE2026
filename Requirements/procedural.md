# Procedural Observations and Implications

This section captures operationally significant events (SIGACTs) that suggest the need for procedural clarification, training, or coordination practices rather than new system capabilities within the current scope of the system. While these observations reveal breakdowns in coordination or shared understanding, analysis indicates that they are more appropriately addressed through operational procedures, role expectations, or communication norms at this stage.

Including these observations separately helps maintain a clear boundary between system requirements and procedural guidance, while preserving traceability to observed events. The procedures described here are not intended to prescribe detailed standard operating procedures, but rather to highlight areas where explicit coordination practices may reduce the likelihood of similar breakdowns.

It is important to note that this classification reflects the current scope of the system under development. Any of these observations may be revisited and reconsidered as system requirements if the scope is expanded in the future to include additional coordination support capabilities.
